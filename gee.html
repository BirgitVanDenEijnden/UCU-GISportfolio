Carbon Sequestration

Carbon Sequestration in De Peel

Using Earth Observation Data, regions with high carbon storage or potential for carbon offset can be identified. This is based on the Gross Primary Production (GPP) and Aboveground Carbon Density (ACD). GPP is the rate at which primary producers in an ecosystem produce chemical energy. ACD is a measure of the amount of carbon stored in the living biomass of trees and other vegetation above the ground. Below is a Google Earth Engine web app of De Peel, a rural area in Noord-Brabant, showing GPP and ACD values. It is a flat region, with villages, forest and agricultural lands. The forest is a mixture of deciduous and coniferous trees, and the villages range from 1000 to 20.000 inhabitants. Like the rest of the Netherlands, this area has an oceanic climate, with mild summers and winters.

Methods and Observations

The values of GPP and ACD for the region of interest were calculated in Google Earth Engine. The areas with high carbon density (ACD) are more suitable for conservation, as these areas already contain high carbon sequestration. From the ACD map above, these regions are the light green areas, which are mainly prevalent around the north west and south east in a fragmented manner. Areas with high GPP and low ACD are promising reforestation areas to increase carbon sequestration. Looking at the two maps above, areas with high GPP coincide with high ACD. However, in the central region, between Heusden, Sluis 13 and Ospeldijk, there is a region with moderate GPP but low ACD. This area could offer a good balance between high primary productivity, and low carbon density, and thus offers potential for reforestation area.
