Geographical Information Systems

Geographical Information Science is the academic field that focuses on the methods, tools and principles involved in understanding, visualizing, and analyzing objects or phenomena based on their geographical location on the Earth's surface. It studies the theoretical foundation behind Geographic Information Systems (GIS), and is aimed at improving GIS data, software and practice. Geographical Information Systems (GIS) are the actual tools and technologies used to manage, analyze, and visualize spatial or geographic data.

GIS can solve complex problems by helping us see patterns, trends, and relationships and predict scenarios, based on spatial data. GIS has applications in various fiels, including environmental analysis, urban planning, disaster management, public health, logistics, and natural resource management. For example, in public health, GIS can track disease spread. In urban planning, GIS can be helpful in determining the best location for new infrastructure based on factors as population density and land use. In environmental analysis, GIS can monitor deforestation and support conservation planning. GIS thus provides a powerful tool to interpet complex datasets and support decision making in various fields.

The course Geographical Information Systems is aimed at providing students with the necessary theory and practical skills needed to create, manage, analyze and generate maps of geospatial data. Topics addressed in the course include data management, spatial analysis, and cartography. The ability to use GIS is highly relevant for administrative purposes by governments, environmental monitoring, land use planning and more.
